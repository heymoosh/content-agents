# The Betrayer

> Name OPEN.

## Core

Eli's first follower, and the one who breaks. Not from greed and not from a change of heart about
the cause, but from exhaustion. He is the burnout made into a person: the dreamer who could not
carry the ambiguity of building the dream and so handed the whole weight of it to Eli, hoping to
live it through him. When it becomes clear there was never a better world waiting at the end of
the destruction, only the next mission and a long procession toward a death he half wants, he is
hollowed out.

## Motivation

He wants the torment to stop. He suspects the enclave life would be a kind of drug, a dulling,
and he would rather have that numbness than the unbearable holding on. He lacks Eli's anger to
propel him and lacks the imagination and thoughtfulness to see that a better path was ever
possible. Hating something gave him momentum but never anything to build toward. Without a light
at the end, the holding on simply runs out.

## The betrayal

He gives the enclaves what they need to mount the counterattack that scatters the rebels. The
horror is that it is understandable. We can relate to it: the burnout of doom news, the
exhaustion of holding an ideal a hostile world keeps rejecting. He believed something good was
going to come of all this and realized, far too late, that it would not.

## Voice

Tired. Funny in a bleak way early on, then less. He talks like someone rationing his words
because speaking costs energy he no longer has. He deflects sincerity.

## Arc

First believer, closest to Eli, slowly emptied. His defection should feel like grief, not
villainy. He is a warning about what the violent path does to the people who are not built like
Eli: it uses them up and leaves nothing.

## What he is not

Not a coward, not a mole, not in it for himself. If a scene makes his betrayal feel like greed or
malice, it is wrong. It is exhaustion.
