# The Unwilling Prince

> Name OPEN. Modeled in spirit on the wealthy heir who made the wealth inequality documentary,
> someone born inside who turned and looked.

## Core

An enclave insider who is complicit but not evil. He knows something is wrong and cannot escape
the logic of his position. He tried to understand the inequality, even to name it, and was met
only with resistance to discussing it at all: justifications dressed as God or natural selection,
or people who simply self exiled rather than answer. Not a hero. Not a villain. Someone whose
exhaustion runs in a different direction from the Betrayer's.

## Motivation

He wants the truth to be sayable inside a world built to keep it unsaid. When that proves
impossible, he chooses to leave rather than keep performing a belief he no longer holds.

## Arc

He leaves before the end. In the timeline of The Last Titan, he and his family lose everything
and have to walk away from it all, and the rebels pick them up. Inside the movement he becomes one
of the main story creators of the future, rich with the insight of having lived inside the
enclave. He can speak from experience about the signs of a society rotting from within, and he has
the storytelling skill to weave that into a narrative that warns. He may give the rebels something
useful on his way out, an act that is neither heroic nor villainous, just human and tired. He
becomes central to the erasure project in Book 2, the one who turns the enclave's self mythology
into altered, fictional warnings.

## Voice

Articulate, self aware, a little haunted. He speaks like someone who has spent years being the
only honest voice in rooms that did not want one, and has learned to say true things quietly.

## What he is not

Not a savior who redeems the enclave from inside, not a turncoat who hates his own people. He is
the witness who survives to tell it.
