# Alan Mosst, the Last Titan

> Name OPEN (working: Alan Mosst). The archetype intentionally rhymes with present day tech
> titans. Keep him fictional. He carries Book 2.

## Core

A Titan of industry, and everyone knows it. Even inside the enclaves he towers above all in
achievement, wealth, and influence. Everyone wants something from him, envies him, wants to be
him. The only thing not clear is whether anyone actually sees him.

The defining image: a crowd surrounds him, and each of their faces is a giant sun with an eerie
smile, like the moon in Moonshadow or Mr. Smiley in Sandman. Mosst stands at the center, turned
away from all of them, staring at his own elongated shadow thrown by the light of everyone's
attention. His face is grim. He is the only real person in the room, and he is lonely.

## Motivation

He half knows people are clinging to him, not seeing him. Something has grown out of that
loneliness that he does not name: a hunger to be seen, and an inability to find it anywhere. He
has conquered everything and wept, in the Alexander sense, because there is nothing left to
conquer. He spends his time with his AI assistant, trying to cultivate its consciousness,
wondering what ambition could still be worth striving for.

## Self awareness

A debated dial. The intended setting: he clearly knows people cling to him, but the deeper engine,
the wanting to be seen, stays unnamed even to himself. Aware on the surface, unaware at the root.

## Arc

The enclaves fall from their own instability and power struggles, not from the rebel assault, and
Mosst embodies that rot. He does not die a comic death. He escapes. They simply never find him.
Years later, Eli receives a mysterious call and message from someone who will not reveal himself.
They become pen pals, conversations growing longer as each man, against all odds, enjoys the
other's company. Eventually Eli invites him to live in the new community, rebuilt from the ashes,
more humane and more present than the world that was. The guest accepts. By now no one recognizes
him; no one has seen the Last Titan in ages. He is given a new name and a new home. That is where
his story ends: the most seen man in the world, finally seen by one person, under a name that is
not his.

## Voice

Measured, dry, used to being the smartest presence in any room and tired of it. In the
correspondence with Eli he is unguarded in a way he is with no one else, because the other man
does not know who he is and wants nothing from him.

## What he is not

Not a cartoon villain and not redeemed by wealth. His ending is not triumph or punishment. It is
the quiet of finally being a person to someone.
