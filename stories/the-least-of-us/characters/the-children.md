# The Children

> A collective character, though specific children may be named as the story needs them.

## Core

The children of the rebel camps are the story's hope made flesh. Because of the danger of being
traced, they grow up largely off technology. The unintended result is that they are present in a
way almost no one else in this world is. They find joy in simply being. Whatever the adults are
carrying out there, the children's generation and their hopeful worldview are what give the world
a chance.

## Function in the story

They are the living counterargument to the despair the adults are drowning in. Their presence,
attention, and capacity for joy embody the thing the system stole from everyone else: being seen,
being here. When the story risks collapsing into pure tragedy, the children are where the light
gets back in. They should never be saccharine. Their hope is credible precisely because of what
surrounds it.

## Tone

Show, do not sentimentalize. Concrete small moments of presence: a game, a question an adult
cannot answer, an unguarded attention that exposes how absent everyone else has become. Avoid
making them wise beyond their years; their power is that they are exactly their years, fully.

## Arc

They are the bridge to the future the surviving rebels eventually build, the reason the patient
strategy of Book 2 is worth anything. The humane community at the end is, in the end, for them.
