I think the last chapter belongs to Lena - the leader's lost love. I don't quite know if it's a conversation they had earlier in their life or one if the last ones they had while she was sick.

She shares a story - a theory rather, of why we're all here.

It's based on a synchronicity experience I had and a moment of - understanding? Realization? Connecting dots? Where I felt everything coming together into a narrative based on what I was experiencing.

I had started wondering whether synchronicity was a kind of influence on probabilities through our consciousness - the highest vibration of consciousness being of love. And if so, then what might happen if more of us lived authentically, in love and honesty, clarifying our consciousness individually and as a collective - can that cause bigger improbabilities to occur?

And if time is apparently not linear but happening all at once, then maybe on some level our perceived future can even affect our past - even our distant past.

What if that collective consciousness pulls the probabilities towards something highly improbable - like the creation of everything that exists in the universe? And that it happens in our future - which somehow affected our past?

But the moment I broke down crying was when I realized that it suggested the world I always wanted to live in - a kinder, empathetic one - was not an impossible ideal, but an inevitable destination. Because everything is already here.

We made it. Somehow we made it.
